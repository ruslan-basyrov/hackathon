## Channel preference per CJ step (% who use dominant channel)

| Step | **Judith** | **Franz** | **Peter** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Info gathering | **58% self-online** | **90% self-online** | **61% customer service** |
| Comparison | **74% self-online** | **94% self-online** | **51% customer service** |
| Consultation | **90% via advisor** | **61% self-online** | **61% customer service** |
| Purchase | **78% via advisor** | **89% self-online** | **59% customer service** |
| Contract questions | 40% self-online | 72% self-online | **63% customer service** |
| Claim reporting | 57% self-online | 85% self-online | **62% customer service** |

**This is the most important table**. The channel preference shows three completely different customer journey logics:

- **Judith**: starts online (info/compare), switches to advisor (consultation/purchase), returns to online (management)
- **Franz**: stays online the entire journey — including consultation and purchase
- **Peter**: stays in customer service the entire journey — including info and comparison

**A single intervention strategy cannot serve all three.**

## Conversion strategy per persona

### Judith (Segment 1 — Rising Hybrids)
- **Drop-off point**: initial price display, when she sees that "better" tariffs require advisor
- **Coach intervention**: surface market comparison, reframe price, offer smooth advisor handoff (she *wants* the human anyway)
- **Conversion definition**: online purchase OR smooth advisor handoff — **both count**
- **Avoid**: aggressive online-only push

### Franz (Segment 2 — Online Affine)
- **Drop-off point**: final price screen when result exceeds initial expectation
- **Coach intervention**: justify price change transparently, offer cheaper-tariff alternative, save-progress feature
- **Conversion definition**: online purchase (advisor handoff is failure)
- **Avoid**: anything that adds friction or implies advisor need

### Peter (Segment 3 — Service Affine)
- **Drop-off point**: very early — overwhelmed by complexity, leaves before tariff selection
- **Coach intervention**: detect overwhelm early, proactively offer callback or service number, simplify the form
- **Conversion definition**: qualified service contact (callback booked, phone clicked) — **online purchase is not the target**
- **Avoid**: pushing self-service, adding more options to a screen

---

## The "do not unify" warning

A naive Conversion Coach that uses the same intervention strategy across all three personas will:
- annoy Franz (who hates handoff suggestions)
- under-serve Peter (who needs immediate handoff, not delayed)
- partially help Judith (who is in the middle and most tolerant)

**Personalization of interventions per detected persona is therefore the central technical challenge of this case.**
